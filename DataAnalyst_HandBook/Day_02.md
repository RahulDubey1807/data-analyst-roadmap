# 📌 Concept 4: Why Do Databases Exist?

## My Understanding
Databases are designed to store, organize and manage large amounts of data efficiently.

Unlike Excel, databases can handle millions of records, allow multiple users to access data simultaneously, retrieve information quickly, maintain relationships between data and reduce redundancy.

---

## Why Is This Important?
Modern companies like Amazon, Flipkart and Netflix generate millions of records every day.

Without databases, storing, searching and managing this data would become slow, inconsistent and unreliable.

Databases make large-scale business operations possible.

---

## Real World Example

Imagine Amazon receives 100,000 orders every hour.
If all orders were stored inside a single Excel file:

• Searching for a customer's order would be slow.

• Multiple employees couldn't safely edit the file simultaneously.

• Duplicate customer information would be stored repeatedly.

Instead, Amazon stores the information inside relational databases where customers, orders and products are stored separately and connected using unique IDs.

---

## Interview Question

Q. Why do companies use databases instead of Excel?

### My Answer

Companies use databases because they can efficiently handle very large amounts of data, support multiple users at the same time, retrieve data quickly, maintain relationships between different tables and ensure data consistency and security.

Excel is useful for small datasets and quick analysis, but databases are designed for enterprise-scale applications.

---

# 💡 Biggest Learning

I realized that databases were not created just to store data.

They were created to solve problems like redundancy, slow searching, multiple users accessing data simultaneously and maintaining relationships between data.

---

## ❌ Common Mistakes

• Thinking Excel and databases serve the same purpose.

• Believing databases are only used because they store more rows.

• Ignoring concepts like redundancy and relationships.
