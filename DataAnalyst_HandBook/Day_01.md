# 📌 Concept 1 : What Does a Data Analyst Actually Do?

## My Understanding

A Data Analyst's primary responsibility is **not** to create dashboards or write SQL queries.

The main responsibility is to solve business problems using data.

A Data Analyst first understands the business problem, asks the right questions, finds the required data, analyzes it and finally provides insights that help stakeholders make better business decisions.

---

## Why Is This Important?

Many beginners think SQL or Power BI is the job.

In reality they are only tools.

The actual job is understanding business problems and converting data into business decisions.

---

## Real World Example

Business Problem:

Yesterday's sales in Gujarat dropped significantly.

A good analyst does NOT immediately write SQL.

Instead, the thinking process is:

## Interview Question

**Q. What does a Data Analyst do?**

### My Answer

A Data Analyst helps businesses make data-driven decisions by understanding business problems, analyzing data and communicating meaningful insights.

# 📌 Concept 2 : Purpose of Every Tool

Instead of remembering software names, remember **why** each tool exists.

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| SQL | Retrieve and answer business questions from stored data |
| Python | Automate tasks and perform advanced analysis |
| Pandas | Clean, transform and analyze datasets |
| Excel | Quick business analysis and reporting |
| Power BI | Present insights visually to stakeholders |

---

# 📌 Concept 3 : The Five Questions Every Data Analyst Answers

## 1. What happened? 
Sales dropped 20%.
---

## 2. Why did it happen?
Inventory became out of stock.
---

## 3. What will happen?
Demand is expected to increase next month.
---

## 4. What should we do?
Increase inventory before Diwali.
---

## 5. Did our decision work?
Sales increased by 18% after inventory was increased.
